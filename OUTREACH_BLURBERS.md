# Blurber Outreach — Email Template & Target List

## How to use this

1. Send the email below to 8–12 people from the target list, **personalising the first sentence** for each. Don't mass-send — agents and authors can smell that.
2. Attach the latest PDF — currently **`versions/More_Solutions_More_Problems_Reader_Edition_v67.pdf`** (246 pages, A4, 3.3 MB).
3. Expected reply rate: 20–30%. Aim for 3–6 yeses out of 10 sends.
4. The window before print is 4 weeks — they need that long to actually read it.

> I'm Ahmed Hafdi, a Moroccan software engineer and researcher. My first book — *More Solutions = More Problems: Why Every Solution Creates the Next Problem* — comes out in 2026. It is the trade edition of a research framework I have been developing on what I call *cascade innovation*: the structural tendency of solutions in connected systems to create problems faster than they are solved.
>
> The book surveys seven domains (mathematics, physics, computer science, economics, medicine, politics, society) and then offers a practical framework — the Cobra Score — for estimating cascade risk before deployment. There is also a chapter that steelmans the strongest objections to my own argument, because I want the book to earn its assent rather than borrow it.

If now is not the moment, I understand entirely — and would be glad to send a copy of the printed edition when it ships.

>

## What "yes" looks like

A blurb is typically 1–3 sentences. Anything from one of these names is gold. If multiple people respond positively, pick the four strongest and put them on the back cover.

## What "no" looks like

Most replies will be polite no's or silence. That is normal. Do not chase. Move on.

## Timing

Send all emails this week. Follow up exactly once, 10–14 days later, with a short note. Then leave them alone. Reading and blurbing takes 3–4 weeks. Plan v50 final assembly for week 5.
