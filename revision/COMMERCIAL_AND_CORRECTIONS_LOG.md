## Corrections applied (from KNOWN_ISSUES.md — the ones safe without external verification)
- **Fake-Einstein epigraph removed.** Ch13's apocryphal "The measure of intelligence is the ability
  to change." → a genuine, on-theme **R. Buckminster Fuller** quote ("…build a new model that makes
  the existing model obsolete.").
- **Duplicate + misattributed quote fixed.** Ch6's "Not everything that can be counted counts…
  (Attributed to Albert Einstein)" — both a wrong attribution *and* a duplicate of Ch11's correctly-
  attributed Cameron epigraph — replaced with a genuine, sourced, on-theme line from **Stiglitz, Sen
  & Fitoussi, *Mismeasuring Our Lives* (2010)**.
- **Antibiotic-resistance figures modernised** everywhere they appeared (intro, Ch7 ×3, Conclusion,
  the historical timeline): now lead with the rigorous **1.27 million direct deaths (2019, Lancet
  GRAM)** instead of the older 700k, and the **"10 million by 2050"** figure is consistently framed as
  a *widely cited but contested projection*, not a fact. (The Ch7 passage that is explicitly about the
  2016 O'Neill review keeps O'Neill's own 700k figure, now correctly tense-marked and paired with the
  newer estimate.)
- **Order-of-magnitude estimates hedged.** "Borlaug saved a billion" → "is credited with helping save
  a billion"; "200 million lives saved" → "an estimated 200 million."
