# Expert Reviewer Brief — *More Solutions = More Problems*

Thank you for reading. The book argues that, in highly interconnected systems, solutions tend to
generate new problems faster than they resolve them — a *tendency*, deliberately not framed as a
proven law. We need domain experts to confirm that the claims **in their field** are defensible and
fairly stated. Please read with a sceptical eye; we would rather hear it from you than from a
reviewer.

## Who we're asking (one per field)
- **Mathematician / theoretical CS** — Chapter 3 (foundations crisis, Gödel/Turing, P vs NP) and the
  framework in Chapter 10 (the combinatorial-growth argument; the NP-completeness / undecidability
  analogies). *Is the math used correctly and not overclaimed?*
- **Economist** — Chapter 6 (Jevons, Goodhart, 2008, QE, trade) and the Cascade Risk Index in Ch11.
- **Physician / public-health researcher** — Chapter 7 (antibiotics, opioids, screening/overdiagnosis,
  DSM). *Are the figures current and the causal claims fair?*
- **Historian / STS scholar** — the case studies and the intellectual-genealogy claims (Merton,
  Perrow, Meadows, Tenner, Taleb) in Ch1/Ch14/appendices.

## Three questions per reader
1. **Accuracy:** Is anything in your field wrong, out-of-date, or stated more strongly than the
   evidence supports? (Specifics, please — chapter/section.)
2. **Fairness:** Does the cascade framing distort your field's record, or omit the decisive
   counter-evidence? (See Chapter 14, "The Case Against This Book" — does it concede enough?)
3. **Overclaim check:** Flag any sentence that still reads as "this is proven" rather than "this is a
   tendency/heuristic." (Phase 1 of the revision removed the worst of these; help us catch the rest.)

## What you do NOT need to do
Copyedit, check every number (a fact-checker is doing that), or assess fields outside your own.

## Logistics
Please return chapter/section-keyed notes. A sign-off of the form "the claims in [my field] are
defensible as stated, with the exceptions noted" is exactly what we need before the book goes out.
