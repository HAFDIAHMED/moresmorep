## One-page synopsis (draft)

**Hook.** Progress keeps a hidden ledger. We record what a solution fixes and forget what it breaks —
until the breakage arrives, larger and later, as someone else's crisis.

**Argument.** In any sufficiently interconnected system, solutions interact; the number of possible
interactions grows far faster than the number of solutions, so the *opportunity* for new problems
compounds, and a non-zero share of those opportunities is realised. Stated as a tendency, not a law,
and made falsifiable.

**Structure.**
- *Part I — The Theory:* the mechanism (complexity, interaction, network amplification) and the
  psychology that hides it (temporal discounting, scope insensitivity, institutional incentives).
- *Part II — The Evidence:* seven domains — mathematics, physics, computer science, economics,
  medicine, politics, and social/ecological systems — each showing the same shape.
- *Part III — The Framework:* how the cascade works and a practical Cascade Risk Index for spotting
  it before deployment (presented as a discipline, not a measuring instrument).
- *Part IV — The Response:* cascade-aware design (Montreal Protocol, smallpox, REACH as cases that
  contained the cascade) and the institutional/education changes it implies.
- *Chapter 14 — The Case Against This Book:* the book argues against itself.

**Audience & comps.** Readers of Tenner (*Why Things Bite Back*), Taleb (*Antifragile*), Meadows
(*Thinking in Systems*), Clearfield & Tilcsik (*Meltdown*), Dan Davies (*The Unaccountability
Machine*).

**Why now.** AI deployment, climate intervention, and platform regulation are all live cascade
decisions; the book gives a vocabulary and a checklist for them.

**Note on length/format.** A "Reader's Edition" (no formal math) exists alongside the underlying
technical paper, supporting both a trade edition and a referenced/expanded edition.
