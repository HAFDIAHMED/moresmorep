# Legal / Defamation Review Checklist — *More Solutions = More Problems*

For a publishing lawyer or experienced legal reviewer. The book makes pointed, named claims about
living people and active companies in a blame/causation context. This is the single largest legal
exposure for a worldwide trade release.

## Priority targets (named parties in critical/causal context)
- **The Sackler family / Purdue Pharma** (opioid chapter + Ch2): statements about marketing,
  concealment, and causal responsibility for the opioid epidemic. Verify each against the public
  record (litigation findings, the 2007 and 2020 federal resolutions). Distinguish proven facts from
  characterisation.
- **Meta / Facebook / Instagram, and Mark Zuckerberg** (Ch5, Ch9): claims about engagement
  algorithms, internal research ("the company had this research and did not act"), and harm to teens.
  Confirm sourcing (e.g., the 2021 disclosures) and phrase as reported/alleged where appropriate.
- **Google, Amazon, Apple** (Ch5/Ch6): monopoly-rent, labour-injury, surveillance claims — confirm
  figures and attribute to regulators/investigations rather than asserting as bare fact.
- **Named individuals** presented as cascade "villains" or via disputed conduct (e.g., Andrew
  Wakefield, Thomas Midgley, individual researchers). Confirm the public record; for Wakefield the
  fraud finding is well-documented (GMC 2010) — cite it.
- **Purdue/Sackler, FTX/Bankman-Fried, Wells Fargo, AIG** etc.: where wrongdoing is adjudicated,
  cite the judgment; where not, use "alleged"/"reported".

## Checklist per named claim
- [ ] Is the statement of fact **true and sourced**, or is it opinion/characterisation?
- [ ] If derogatory and factual, is the source strong enough to defend? (court finding > investigation
      > journalism > advocacy report.)
- [ ] Are living individuals named where the company alone would suffice?
- [ ] Is alleged conduct flagged as alleged where not adjudicated?
- [ ] Jurisdiction: UK/EU libel exposure is higher than US — the book is aimed worldwide. Apply the
      stricter standard.
- [ ] Quotations attributed to real people — accurate and in context? (cross-check with the
      fact-checker's ATTRIB findings.)

## Output
A marked-up list: KEEP / REPHRASE (with suggested wording) / REMOVE, per named-party claim, plus any
required disclaimers. Feed REPHRASE items back for a targeted edit pass.
