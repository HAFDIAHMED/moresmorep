The four visual rhymes

Frame 1 ↔ Frame 8 ↔ Frame 15 · black with serif text · the trailer's only three moments of stillness
Frame 2 ↔ Frame 9 · identical wide composition · different century · the structural argument of the entire film
Frame 4 ↔ Frame 10 ↔ Frame 12 · three macro top-down transactions · coin, pill, signature · same composition, different solution
Frame 7 ↔ Frame 13 · close-up · same framing · black shirt → white shirt · 1880 → 1995
How to shoot in one evening:
Frames 2, 5, 9 — locked-off wide. Set the camera once. Don't move it across the shoot.
Frames 3, 7, 13 — same chair, same lens, two wardrobe changes. Shoot all three back-to-back.
Frames 4, 10, 12 — all top-down inserts. Same table, same light. Shoot in a single 10-minute block.
Frame 6 — basement, garage, or any dark space. Add cage props (or a single barred window cast as shadow).
Frames 14, 15 — book held against any neutral wall · then a black slide in editing.

Total shoot time: one evening of three hours, if everything is set up in advance.
Total edit time: two evenings in CapCut · pace the cuts to the music.
